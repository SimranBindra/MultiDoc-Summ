[1] The Queen of TV comedy, Lucille Ball, died on April 26, 1989, from a ruptured abdominal aorta
[2] President Bush, former President Reagan, the president of CBS, American comedians and actors, neighbors, residents of her hometown, television critics, and just plain fans paid tributes to her
[3] Her burial was private, but more than 2,000 attended three memorial services held on May 8
[4] Bob Hope, a frequent co-star and friend, created a TV special in her honor
[5] Two of her surviving TV co-stars, Gail Gordon and Keith Thibodeaux, remembered her fondly
[6] She is best remembered for her physical and verbal comedy and superb timing
[7] She was born in 1911 in Jamestown, N.Y., and, after staring in high school plays, became a model and chorus girl, moving in 1933 to Hollywood
[8] There, she was in over 75 movies, usually playing the second banana
[9] In 1940 she married Desi Arnaz, and together they revolutionized television comedy with "I Love Lucy" which started in 1951
[10] Filmed with three cameras before a live audience, it became a rerun gold mine and has been seen in 80 countries
[11] After her divorce, she remarried and continued to be a successful television entrepreneur and star of television and screen.
