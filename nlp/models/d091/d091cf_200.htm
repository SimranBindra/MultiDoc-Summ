[1] Flash floods raged through small towns along the Ohio River in eastern Ohio on Thursday
[2] Death toll from the flood was put at ten initially, but was raised to 21 as more bodies were found
[3] The number may still go higher since little hope remains for the fourteen still unaccounted for
[4] The floods hit two creeks near Shadyside, Ohio, about 10 miles south of Wheeling, West Virginia
[5] Five and a half inches of rain fell in slightly more than three hours
[6] The flood created a wall of water 15 to 20 feet high as it tore through the area
[7] The force of the raging water demolished a crowded tavern in Shadyside
[8] A nine-year-old girl who was swept into the creek and on into the Ohio River miraculously survived a seven mile ordeal by clinging to logs
[9] Ohio Governor Celeste declared a state of emergency after surveying the area
[10] The National Guard was brought in to patrol and help rebuild roads
[11] Dog teams from four states were used in searching for survivors and bodies
[12] Few homeowners had flood insurance to aid in rebuilding lost or damaged homes
[13] The Federal Emergency Management Agency planned to accept low-interest loan and grant applications.
