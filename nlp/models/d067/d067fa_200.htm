[1] Hurricane Andrew hit the Bahamas, southern Florida and Louisiana in August 1992 with winds ranging up to 165 miles per hour, killing 2 in the Bahamas, 15 in Florida, and 2 in Louisiana
[2] The greatest property damage was in Florida where a quarter of a million people were left homeless and 2 million were without power
[3] Property damage there exceeded $15 billion, most of it uninsured
[4] An early estimate set the total cost of the storm to insurers at more than $8 billion, but others forecast a total as high as $20 billion, mainly to U.S. insurers
[5] After local officials in Florida criticized delays in delivery of emergency supplies, the White House promised "dramatic" improvement within 24 hours and President Bush ordered troops to Florida
[6] The U.S. government estimated it would cost $20 billion to $30 billion to rebuild Florida and care for those displaced by the storm
[7] Fortunately Andrew did not hit low-lying New Orleans or off--shore oil facilities along the Gulf coast
[8] After the hurricane had passed an article in the "Financial Times" compared Andrew to a crisis in the value of the U.S. dollar which was "blowing itself out" during the course of the hurricane
[9] The comparison was inept.
