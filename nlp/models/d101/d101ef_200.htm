[1] Soviet hard-liners attempted a coup in order to thwart the impending signing of the new Union Treaty incorporating the reforms of Soviet President Gorbachev
[2] The coup leaders put Gorbachev under house arrest in the Crimea
[3] Russian President Boris Yeltsin led opposition to the coup, which did not have popular support, and it was put down after three days
[4] Gorbachev was returned to Moscow and was reinstated as president
[5] Some observers wondered about Gorbachev's actions just before the coup attempt since he had ignored ample warnings that it might happen
[6] Conspiracy buffs even wondered if Gorbachev might have even been part of the planning
[7] Upon his return to power, Gorbachev ordered the arrest of the coup leaders and made new appointments to key positions
[8] The putdown of the coup meant that the reforms giving more authority to the republics would go forward
[9] Gorbachev and Yeltsin approached the reforms differently
[10] Gorbachev wanted to work through the Communist Party, but Yeltsin had quit the party and wanted to see its influence lessened
[11] Yeltsin was clearly more in touch with the people
[12] The two leaders made a joint appearance at the Russian Parliament where it became increasingly clear that Gorbachev's authority was waning while Yeltsin's was ascending.
