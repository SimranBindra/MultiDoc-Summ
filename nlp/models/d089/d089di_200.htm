[1] A powerful cold front moved through the eastern half of the United States on November 15 and 16, carving a deadly path of destruction
[2] The abnormally warm air preceding the front spawned tornados, violent thunderstorms, and heavy rainfall
[3] Tornados struck in at least seven states
[4] Hardest hit was Huntsville, Alabama, where a tornado roared down a major street near rush hour, leveling a shopping district, apartment development, and elementary school
[5] Seventeen were killed, 463 injured, and 1000 left homeless
[6] Witnesses recall a huge roar, water and winds, screaming, hiding, and helping others
[7] Roads were blocked and power and telephone lines were cut
[8] Rescuers, including National Guard troops, worked all night sifting through the rubble
[9] Damages were estimated at $100 million, with about $1 million needed to provide emergency clothing and temporary housing
[10] President Bush declared Huntsville a disaster area
[11] Marilyn Quayle, wife of the Vice President, toured the area with Alabama's governor and senators on November 18
[12] Near Newburgh, New York high winds caused an elementary school cafeteria to cave in on November 16
[13] Seven children were killed and 18 injured
[14] A truck driver also was killed in New Jersey and a woman died in Illinois
[15] Injuries occurred in five other states.
