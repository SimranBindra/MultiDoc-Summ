[1] John Lennon spirit is still very much alive to adoring fans worldwide ten years after his death
[2] His 50th birthday was observed with a global celebration of his peace anthem "Imagine" which was played for an estimated 1 billion people in 130 countries
[3] The song was played following a brief ceremony at the United Nations
[4] Fans also gathered in Liverpool, Moscow, Los Angles and New York to pay tribute to the former Beatle
[5] One biographer described Lennon as an anorexic bisexual addled by drugs who raged his way from Liverpool to New York, but friends and family denounced that version of Lennon's life
[6] Lennon himself acknowledged his drug use and sexual promiscuity, although he claimed that his worst days were behind him
[7] His wife, Yoko Ono admitted using heroin for a time
[8] He had also been portrayed as a dour, shoeless youth with an unfit mother, a description refuted by his half-sister in her book
[9] Since his death, his wife has kept his legacy alive, issuing new albums of his work and selling memorabilia, featuring his image
[10] Lennon was gunned down by a deranged fan outside his Manhattan apartment December 8, 1980 at the age of 40.
