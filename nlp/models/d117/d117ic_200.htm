[1] The Booker Prize was first awarded in 1969
[2] It honors the best work of fiction by a British or Commonwealth author published in Britain during the preceding 12 months
[3] The Booker is now the best known and most prestigious fiction award in Britain
[4] It carries a substantial monetary prize, and is administered by Book Trust, an educational charity
[5] Booker, an international food and farming business, sponsors the Prize
[6] British publishers can submit up to three titles for consideration
[7] Each year a management team selects a new panel of judges
[8] They announce a list of six finalists
[9] Bookmakers establish betting odds, and heavy betting ensues, most of it by literary pundits
[10] Interest builds in the period leading up to the announcement of the winner, usually in October
[11] The hype that accompanies the selection boosts book sales
[12] Penelope Lively's "Moon Tigers" won in 1987
[13] Peter Carey's "Oscar and Lucinda" won in 1988
[14] Kazuo Ishiguro's "The Remains of the Day" won in 1989
[15] Antonia Byatt's "Possession" won in 1990
[16] Michael Ondaatje's "The English Patient" won in 1992, and John Kelman's "How Late It Is, How Late" won in 1994
[17] In 1994 the short-list was severely criticized, and a hypothetical 1894 Booker was contested.
