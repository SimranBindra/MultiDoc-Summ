[1] Floods being called the worst in 100 years have hit major parts of Guangdong, Guangxi, Hunan, Jiangxi, Fujian and Zhejiang Provinces
[2] They were caused by torrential rains in the Xiangjiang, Xunjiang, Xijiang and Beijiang river valleys
[3] Cities most seriously affected where Changsha, Zhuzhou and Xiangtan in Hunan Province, Wuzhou in Guangxi Province and Sanming in Fujian Province
[4] At the Changsha city monitoring station, the water level rose to 38.93 meters, some 0.56 meters above the previous record
[5] 500,000 soldiers and civilians fought to contain the floodwaters there
[6] In Wuzhou city, the Xunjiang River's floodwater speed was recorded at 46,200 cubic-meters-per-second, the most ferocious since the nation's founding in 1949
[7] In Guangzhou Province 200 people died, 189,000 rooms were destroyed, and 1.2 million hectares of crops were ruined
[8] Economic losses were set at 10.2 billion yuan
[9] Many local, provincial and Central Party members provided support and expressed their concern, including Chairman Jiang Zemin and Premier Li Peng
[10] Noteworthy were the efforts of Central Party official Wen Jiabao
[11] He visited Changsha, Zhuzhou, Xiangtan and Jiangxi cities to offer appreciation, encouragement and guidance for the ongoing flood fighting and relief work, and to direct the resumption of industrial and agricultural production.
